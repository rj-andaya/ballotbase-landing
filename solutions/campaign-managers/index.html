Does This Sound Familiar?

Running a campaign is hard enough without having to be a data analyst too.

You don't know what your opponent is doing financially
Your opponent just filed their quarterly report. You found out three days later from a news article. By then they'd already made moves you didn't see coming. You needed that information on filing day, not a week after.
Your burn rate is a gut feeling, not a number
You know roughly how much you've raised and roughly how much you've spent. But cost-to-raise, cash runway, and spend efficiency? Those live in a spreadsheet someone updates when they have time. That's not a financial strategy. That's a guess.
Your donor base has blind spots
Are your donors concentrated in one zip code? One employer? Are your biggest givers already maxed out? Are people in your district giving to your opponent too? These questions have answers. Most campaigns just never look.
