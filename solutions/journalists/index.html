Does This Sound Familiar?

The FEC database has everything. Getting to it is the problem.

FEC data is public but painful
The filings are there. The data is technically accessible. But navigating raw FEC exports, cross-referencing committee IDs, and making sense of Schedule A records takes hours of work that most reporters don't have on a breaking news timeline. By the time you've pulled it together, the story has moved.
IE spending is scattered across dozens of filings
Independent expenditure activity tells some of the most important stories in a race. But it's spread across multiple committee filings, updated on inconsistent schedules, and nearly impossible to aggregate quickly. Most coverage skips it entirely because it's too hard to pull on deadline.
Donor pattern stories require cross-referencing you can't do manually
The Chevron donor showing up in two climate candidates' filings. The real estate developer maxed out to a housing affordability champion. These stories exist in the data. Finding them manually across hundreds of filings isn't realistic.

Fast access to the data behind the story.

Search the Full Donor Database

Platform vs. Money, Automatically
BallotBase scrapes candidate websites for actual policy language and compares it against their observed donor patterns. When a candidate's financial relationships don't match their platform, BallotBase flags it. Data vs. data, not opinion. Every finding is cited to a committee ID and report type.

Outside Spending, All in One Place
Track every independent expenditure active in a race. Which organizations are spending, for or against which candidate, how much, when, and in what format. IE activity mapped across the full campaign timeline and updated each filing cycle.
