Platform, in their words

Summarized from the campaign's own materials — how the candidate describes their positions, not our assessment. Source: the campaign's website.

Eric Guerra's campaign centers on public safety, affordable housing, and support for working families in Sacramento County District 1. His priorities include converting homelessness through housing development, expanding childcare access, protecting renters, combating human trafficking, and advocating for immigrant communities.

Housing Affordability
Prioritize converting vacant lots into affordable housing and expanding shelter services to address homelessness without supporting park encampments.
“Led converting vacant lots with homeless camps into lasting housing, like 700 affordable units on Stockton Blvd; expanded St. John's women's shelter three-fold.”
Public Safety Policing
Support law enforcement efforts to crack down on human trafficking and prioritize public safety in neighborhoods.
“Led effort to crack down on human trafficking in Sacramento, allowing law enforcement to go after abusers and help trafficking victims.”
Economy Jobs
Support affordable childcare expansion and job training to help working families afford to live in Sacramento.
“Secured $1,000,000 in funding for childcare center closings, start new centers, and apprentice program to train new caregivers.”
Immigration
Advocate for fair treatment of vulnerable immigrant community members through legal services and support.
“Led effort to create FUEL Network offering free immigration legal consultation, mental health services, rapid response hotline and educator training.”
Reproductive Rights
Strong advocate for reproductive rights and dignified access to reproductive healthcare services.
“Wrote law protecting patients from harassment at Planned Parenthood facilities, because everyone deserves dignified access to healthcare.”
Labor Workers Rights
Protect tenants and working families from excessive rent increases and unfair evictions.
“Worked to pass Tenant Protection and Relief Act preventing excessive rent increases and protecting tenants from unfair evictions.”
