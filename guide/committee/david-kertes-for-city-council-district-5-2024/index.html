Platform, in their words

Summarized from the campaign's own materials, how the candidate describes their positions, not our assessment. Source: the campaign's website.

David Kertes is running for Santa Clara Mayor on a 'Family Community First' platform emphasizing public safety, fiscal responsibility, and community investment. His priorities include expanding youth recreation programs, supporting seniors, maintaining police and fire services with improved coverage, and revitalizing downtown with cultural events.

Public Safety Policing
Maintain strong police and fire services with expanded coverage, including building a new Fire Station on the Northside to improve response times.
“Maintain strong public safety while expanding coverage where needed. Prioritize building a Fire Station on the Northside to improve response times and coverage.”
Taxes Fiscal Policy
Pursue responsible spending and fiscal responsibility, making smart budget decisions that strengthen the city without waste.
“We must be responsible with taxpayer dollars, making smart decisions that strengthen our city without waste. A city budget that works smarter, spends wisely.”
Government Accountability
Bring integrity, honesty, and transparency to city government with respectful debates focused on residents' best interests.
“My mission is to bring back integrity, honesty, and transparency to Santa Clara. We should have respectful healthy debates that do not point fingers.”
Social Security Retirement
Support older adults through extended hours for senior programs, lower program costs based on income, and a new Senior Center on the Northside.
“Extended hours for senior programs and facilities and lower program costs to ensure participation is not limited by income. A new Senior Center on the Northside.”
Education
Invest in youth by expanding recreation access, expanding STEM and library after-school programs, and creating dedicated safe spaces for teens.
“Invest in youth by expanding access to recreation, safe spaces, and affordable programs. STEM: Expanding accessibility and Library after school programs. A dedicated Rec Center.”
Infrastructure Transportation
Revitalize downtown and strengthen neighborhoods through public spaces including night markets, outdoor dining, and community events.
“Night markets, outdoor dining, and more dancing in the park to create vibrant public spaces. Downtown revitalization, giving residents a vibrant gathering place.”
