AB 736, explained

Transfer taxes: limitation.

Active In Committee Process · Author: Mark GonzÃ¡lez

In plain English

This bill would limit how much local governments can charge in transfer taxes on real estate sales. Starting January 1, 2027, cities and counties could not charge a combined transfer tax rate higher than 1.5% of the property's sale price. The bill would also waive transfer taxes on the first sale of single-family homes within 5 years after a natural disaster destroys or damages homes on that property.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the bill's official digest. No predictions, no opinions.

  • Local governments could not collect transfer taxes that add up to more than 1.5% of the property sale price, starting January 1, 2027
  • If a local government's current combined transfer tax rate exceeds 1.5%, it would have to reduce or eliminate some of its transfer taxes
  • Single-family homes would not be subject to transfer tax on their first sale if the sale happens within 5 years after a natural disaster damaged or destroyed housing units on that property
  • Local officials would have new responsibilities to implement and enforce these tax limits
