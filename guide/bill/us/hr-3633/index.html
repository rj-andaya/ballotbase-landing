HR 3633, explained

Digital Asset Market Clarity Act

Active Placed on Senate Legislative Calendar under General Orders. Calendar No. 423. · Author: J. Hill (R-AR)

In plain English

This bill creates rules for trading digital assets that use blockchain technology. It says the Commodity Futures Trading Commission would oversee most of these trades, while the Securities and Exchange Commission would handle certain types of brokers and exchanges. Digital assets on mature blockchains could avoid some registration requirements if they meet certain conditions.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • The Commodity Futures Trading Commission would regulate digital commodity exchanges, brokers, and dealers
  • Digital commodities could trade on exchanges if their blockchain is mature and decentralized, or if the issuer files certain reports
  • Digital commodities on mature blockchains would be exempt from SEC registration if annual sales are below a certain amount and other requirements are met
  • Digital commodity exchanges, brokers, and dealers would have to follow anti-money laundering rules under the Bank Secrecy Act
  • Exchanges and brokers would have to keep records and monitor trades according to standards set in the bill
