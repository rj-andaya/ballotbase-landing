S 2296, explained

National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2026

Active Held at the desk. · Author: Roger Wicker (R-MS)

In plain English

This bill sets rules and spending limits for the Department of Defense and Department of Energy for the year 2026. It decides what weapons and equipment the military can buy, how many active duty and reserve soldiers there can be, which military construction projects can happen, and makes changes to certain military workplace programs.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • The Defense Department would be required to create a plan about how new biotechnology affects national security
  • The Navy would have to follow new processes to improve how its surface ships are kept in good condition and ready for combat at private shipyards
  • The Pacific Deterrence Initiative—a program to strengthen U.S. military presence in the Indo-Pacific region—would continue through 2026
  • Various federal rules about diversity, equity, and inclusion programs within the Defense Department would be removed, including the position of Chief Diversity Officer
  • The bill would approve money for specific military construction projects and extend some projects approved in previous years
