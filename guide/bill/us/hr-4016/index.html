HR 4016, explained

Department of Defense Appropriations Act, 2026

Active Motion to proceed to consideration of measure made in Senate. (CR S8522) · Author: Ken Calvert (R-CA)

In plain English

This bill sets aside money for the Department of Defense to spend in 2026 on military activities like paying soldiers, maintaining equipment, buying weapons, and research. It also provides money for military health programs, the CIA retirement fund, and intelligence agencies. The bill includes rules for how these organizations can use the money.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • The Department of Defense would receive appropriated funds for military personnel, operations, equipment purchases, and research and development in fiscal year 2026
  • Money would be allocated to the Defense Health Program, chemical weapons destruction, drug interdiction activities, and the Defense Inspector General
  • Funds would go to the CIA Retirement and Disability System Fund and the Intelligence Community Management Account
  • Rules and restrictions included in this bill and other appropriations acts would apply to how all these funds can be spent
