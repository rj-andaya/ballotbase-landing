HR 4553, explained

Energy and Water Development and Related Agencies Appropriations Act, 2026

Active Read the second time. Placed on Senate Legislative Calendar under General Orders. Calendar No. 156. · Author: Charles Fleischmann (R-TN)

In plain English

This bill decides how much money the U.S. government will spend in 2026 on water projects, energy programs, and related agencies. It provides funding for things like the Army Corps of Engineers, water management in the West, the Department of Energy, and energy regulatory agencies.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • Money would be distributed to the Army Corps of Engineers for civil works projects, including flood control, maintenance, and investigations.
  • The Department of Interior would receive funding for water projects in Utah and the Bureau of Reclamation.
  • The Department of Energy would receive funding for programs covering renewable energy, nuclear energy, fossil fuels, power grids, energy research, and defense-related nuclear activities.
  • Independent agencies like the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission and Nuclear Regulatory Commission would receive appropriated funds.
  • Rules and restrictions would apply to how these funds can be used across all the programs covered by this bill.
