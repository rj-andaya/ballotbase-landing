HR 1, explained

An act to provide for reconciliation pursuant to title II of H. Con. Res. 14.

Active Became Public Law No: 119-21. · Author: Jodey Arrington (R-TX)

In plain English

HR 1 is a budget bill that changes federal taxes, spending, and debt limits across many government programs. The parts shown here would change how SNAP (food assistance) benefits are calculated and who has to meet work requirements to receive them.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • USDA would be prohibited from changing SNAP benefit amounts based on updating what foods are in the Thrifty Food Plan. Benefit amounts could only increase based on the Consumer Price Index (a measure of inflation).
  • Adults ages 55-65 who do not have dependents would be required to meet additional work requirements to keep SNAP benefits beyond 3 months in a 36-month period.
  • Parents and household members would have to meet these work requirements once their child turns age 14 (currently parents with any child under 18 are exempt).
  • Homeless individuals, veterans, and people ages 24 and under who aged out of foster care would be required to meet these work requirements (currently they are exempt).
  • States would have less ability to temporarily suspend the three-month benefit time limit in areas with high unemployment or job shortages.
