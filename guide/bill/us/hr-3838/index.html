HR 3838, explained

Streamlining Procurement for Effective Execution and Delivery and National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2026

Active Received in the Senate. · Author: Mike Rogers (R-AL)

In plain English

This bill sets rules and spending plans for the Department of Defense, Department of Energy defense programs, and Maritime Administration for the year 2026. It decides what military equipment can be bought or built, how much money goes to different programs, and creates policies for military personnel, health care, purchasing, and cybersecurity.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • The Department of Defense would be allowed to acquire or modify military items such as aircraft according to the rules set in this bill.
  • Research, development, testing, and evaluation activities would follow the policies and receive the funding amounts this bill authorizes.
  • Active duty and reserve military personnel levels would be set at the numbers specified in this bill.
  • The Department of Defense would follow new policies regarding how it buys goods and services, including rules about contracting and small business participation.
  • The Department of Defense would operate under new policies for cybersecurity, cyber workforce training, and artificial intelligence activities.
