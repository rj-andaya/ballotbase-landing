HR 471, explained

Fix Our Forests Act

Active Committee on Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry Subcommittee on Conservation, Forestry, Natural Resources, and Biotechnology. Hearings held. With printed Hearing: S.Hrg. 119-27. · Author: Bruce Westerman (R-AR)

In plain English

This bill sets up new rules for managing forests on federal land to reduce wildfire risk. It creates a system to identify high-risk fire areas and speeds up the approval process for forest management projects. It also limits how much courts can do if someone sues over these forest projects.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • The Forest Service and U.S. Geological Survey would jointly create a Fireshed Center to track and predict fire risks in designated high-risk forest areas.
  • Forest management projects in these areas would go through a faster approval process under environmental and species protection laws.
  • Certain forest management activities would be exempt from the environmental review process (NEPA) that normally applies.
  • Courts would have limits on what remedies they could order if someone sued over fireshed management projects.
  • The government would fund forest restoration work, community wildfire risk reduction, and support for wildland firefighters and their families.
