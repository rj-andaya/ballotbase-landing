HR 7148, explained

Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2026

Active Became Public Law No: 119-75. · Author: Tom Cole (R-OK)

In plain English

This bill provides money for several federal departments and agencies for the rest of 2026. It includes funding for the military, labor, health, education, transportation, housing, state department, and other agencies. It also continues funding for the Department of Homeland Security through mid-February 2026 and extends various programs that were set to expire.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • Money would be provided to the Department of Defense, Department of Labor, Department of Health and Human Services, Department of Education, Department of Transportation, Department of Housing and Urban Development, and other listed agencies for 2026.
  • The Department of Homeland Security would receive continuing funding through February 13, 2026, at 2025 funding levels.
  • Several programs set to expire would be extended, including the National Flood Insurance Program, Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF), the Transportation Security Administration's screening services program, and various cybersecurity programs.
  • Programs related to agriculture, trade, immigration, health care, and other areas listed in the bill would continue operating under extended authorities.
