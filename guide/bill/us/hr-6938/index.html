HR 6938, explained

Commerce, Justice, Science; Energy and Water Development; and Interior and Environment Appropriations Act, 2026

Active Became Public Law No: 119-74. · Author: Tom Cole (R-OK)

In plain English

This bill sets aside federal money for fiscal year 2026 (October 2025 through September 2026) for several government departments and agencies. The money covers the Department of Commerce, Department of Justice, NASA, the National Science Foundation, Army Corps of Engineers projects, Department of Energy, Department of the Interior, Environmental Protection Agency, Forest Service, Indian Health Service, and related agencies. The bill also includes rules about how this money can be used.

If this passes

What would actually change, according to the official CRS summary. No predictions, no opinions.

  • Federal agencies listed in the bill would receive appropriated funds for FY2026 operations and programs
  • Requirements and restrictions on how federal agencies spend appropriated money would be set forth in the bill
  • Funding would cover specific activities: commerce, law enforcement, science, space exploration, energy, water development, public lands management, and environmental protection
  • The bill would combine three separate appropriations bills into one, covering Commerce/Justice/Science; Energy and Water; and Interior/Environment
