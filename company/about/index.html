About

Political money is public.
Making sense of it shouldn't take a research team.

BallotBase turns the scattered, hard-to-read public record of political money into one clear system, so anyone tracking legislation or campaigns can see the whole board, not just the pieces a big shop can afford to assemble.

RJ Andaya · Founder

Built by a systems person, not a political operation.

BallotBase is built by RJ Andaya, a Sacramento-based founder who has spent over a decade in revenue operations, the unglamorous discipline of taking scattered, messy data from a dozen systems and turning it into something a team can actually act on.

He immigrated from the Philippines at eleven and grew up in low-income housing. That's where the throughline of his work started: making information that's fragmented, painful, or hidden in plain sight easier to access, understand, and use.

Campaign finance is exactly that problem. The disclosures are already public, but they're split across state portals, federal databases, and city-clerk filings, in formats built for compliance, not clarity. BallotBase is the system that joins them, so the full money picture behind a bill or a race is one query instead of a month of manual work.

Where we stand

Nonpartisan by construction.

BallotBase runs on official government disclosures, the same filings, held to the same methodology, for every candidate, committee, and donor, regardless of party. The data doesn't take a side. It tells you where the money is.

We built it that way on purpose. Lobbying firms, campaigns, and advocacy groups across the spectrum all rely on the same public record, and they should all be able to rely on the same tools to read it. BallotBase is infrastructure, not advocacy. If a number is on BallotBase, it traces back to an official source.

What we believe

Three commitments the product is built on.

📖
Transparency
The money in politics is already public. Our job isn't to expose it, it's to make it legible. We join what the government already discloses into one picture anyone can read.
🎯
Rigor
Every figure traces to an official government filing. No estimates dressed up as facts, no third-party guesses. When entities are hard to match, we'd rather show two records than wrongly merge them into one.
⚖️
Neutrality
Same data, same methodology, all sides. A tool you can trust regardless of who you're working for, because it reports the record, it doesn't argue with it.
